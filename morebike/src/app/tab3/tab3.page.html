Destination Neighborhoods

Pareto Charts

These graphs have the objective of analyzing if the Pareto principle (also known as the 80/20 rule) is fulfilled in the monthly trips per neighborhood as the destination of trips in different years.


It can be seen which are the neighborhoods that contribute the highest proportion of trips on weekdays and on weekends, which make up 80% of the total trips per month.

Boxplots

These graphs show the distribution of trips in the different neighborhoods of the system on weekdays and weekends, as well as those neighborhoods that are classified as atypical for having a considerably higher proportion of trips and that are shown in the points above the upper whisker of each box and schedule.

Mobility between Neighborhoods

In these bar charts we can identify which neighborhoods people come from, ending their trips in the neighborhoods with the highest number of trips at different times, either on weekdays or on weekends.


It is possible to identify some patterns, such as: Local Mobility, Unidirectional Mobility and Bidirectional Mobility between neighborhoods with most registered trips.

Regular Mobility

In this line graph, it is possible to analyze the number of trips registered at different hours of the day, thus knowing the times at which trips usually end in each neighborhood and on the different days of the week.


Thanks to this graph, we can perform an analysis on the demand for bike stations, and identify the places where more dock spaces are required at a certain time of day.


The ECOBICI service is active from 5:00 am to 12:30 am every day.
